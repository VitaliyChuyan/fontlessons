How to improve the result in a 5 km race

Follow these 9 life hacks to increase your abilities and show the result you are truly capable of

One of the most popular formats for beginner runners is the 5km race. This distance is often included in a regular fitness regimen. As soon as you master 5 km, reduce the running time, minute by minute, second by second.

To help you conquer 5 km, we share tips that will reduce your running time.

1. Run for 2 and 10 km

Despite your assumptions, to improve the result by 5 km, it is not necessary to constantly run this distance. Changing distances increases cardio capabilities and makes the heart work better. Mix 5km runs with longer (7.5/10km) and shorter (2/3km) runs to improve endurance and speed.

Combine long and short runs

2. Add high-intensity interval training

If you want to improve your 5K time, you don't have to wind them up on the treadmill all the time. Make friends with high-intensity interval training (HIIT). In layman's terms, this is vigorous exercise (such as sprinting in place or cycling at maximum capacity) with short rest breaks. Although they take less time (just a few minutes per week), studies show that they are more beneficial for cardiovascular endurance training than long, continuous workouts.

Add high-intensity interval training

3. Better slowly, but regularly

Paradox: to run faster, you need to learn to run slowly but regularly. Slow jogging is an effective way to polish your fitness. And while the temptation to give them up is high, they're important for recovery and endurance, as well as for balancing more intense workouts.

4. Think about the pulse

To be ready to run for a long time, try to monitor your heart rate. Instead of focusing on the same pace, do small accelerations. Many fitness gurus believe that this will have a positive effect on the time result. Add 5-minute intervals with acceleration to your standard training and increase them to 10 minutes over time.

Interval training

5. Conquer the height

Conquering peaks is a great skill. Instead of thinking about where to find the energy to do it, imagine that you are squatting and lifting the weight - that's how the muscles feel when you go up. Vary your usual 5km route with some elevation gain, and climbing the stairs home will seem like a pleasant walk.

Climbing up has a good effect on the result

6. Strong leg muscles = fast running

Leg muscles are your main tool. No wonder if you want to get them in shape. The main thing is not to forget about the hips and buttocks, which are often ignored (although for good reason, they help a lot when running up hills). Work all your leg muscles in the gym to get maximum results.

Train your legs

7. Try different surfaces and repeat

Concrete, gravel, flat or uneven surfaces - get used to different surfaces, it will make you more prepared. Of course, it is not a fact that you will want to repeat any of this, but you will know what to expect, where to save energy, and where it is better to give it your best.

8. Train in good weather

If you've ever run in the winter, you may notice that breathing in cold air puts more strain on your lungs. Train in milder weather to be sure of your time and endurance in normal conditions.

Run in good weather

9. Compete with your friends

Nothing gets you going like healthy competition. Invite your friends to the track on Sunday morning and tell them that the loser pays for breakfast together. Wow, this "wall of runners" just disappeared.

Train with friends
